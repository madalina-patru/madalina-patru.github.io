Research

Work in progress

My research studies how public policies affect household wealth, homeownership, and income distribution. I focus on the role of property taxation in shaping housing markets across generations and on the distributional income effects of fiscal policy in the euro area.

Research Interests

Sustainable finance, banking regulation, systemic risk, property taxes, and distributional effects of macroeconomic policies.

Work in Progress

Passing the Keys: How Public Policy Can Shape Intergenerational Homeownership

with Melina Ludolph and Lena Tonzer
This paper studies how property taxation affects homeownership across generations. Exploiting Italy's 2012 IMU reform as a natural experiment, we analyse whether higher recurrent property taxes can affect households differentially based on their age and reshape the intergenerational distribution of homeownership.

Beyond Multipliers: Distributional Income Effects of Government Spending in the Euro Area

with Gökan Ider, Alexander Kriwoluzky, Melina Ludolph, and Lena Tonzer
Using harmonised micro-level data from the EU-SILC, this project examines how fiscal policy affects income growth across the income distribution, as well as across gender and occupational groups. We investigate the channels through which different types of government spending — consumption versus investment — transmit to household incomes.

Past theses: ESG Impact on Financial Performance: Exploring Factor Models Across Different Economic Conditions (M.Sc., Ca' Foscari, 2023); The Role of the ECB in the Green Transition (B.Sc., Ca' Foscari, 2021).
