Research

Current projects and working papers

My research lies at the intersection of financial economics and public policy. I study how macroeconomic and regulatory policies affect households and financial institutions, with a focus on distributional consequences, sustainable finance, and systemic risk.

Current Projects

Impact of Property Taxes on Household Wealth and Homeownership

Empirical analysis of fiscal reform effects on asset distribution and tenure decisions. This project examines how changes in property taxation influence household wealth accumulation and the decision to own versus rent.

Distributional Consequences of Macroeconomic Policies

Examining inequality and heterogeneity in the transmission of policy shocks across income groups. This project investigates how monetary and fiscal policy measures affect different segments of the income distribution.
