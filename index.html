Madalina Patru

Doctoral Researcher in Economics · IWH Halle

Welcome! I am a Doctoral Researcher in the Department of Financial Markets at the Halle Institute for Economic Research (IWH) in Germany. My research studies how public policies — from property taxation to government spending — affect household wealth, homeownership, and income distribution across different groups.

Research Interests

Sustainable finance, banking regulation, systemic risk, property taxes, and distributional effects of macroeconomic policies.

Current Projects

Passing the Keys: How Public Policy Can Shape Intergenerational Homeownership

with Melina Ludolph and Lena Tonzer
This paper studies how property taxation affects homeownership across generations. Exploiting Italy's 2012 IMU reform as a natural experiment, we analyse whether higher recurrent property taxes — through their capitalisation into house prices — can lower entry barriers for younger, liquidity-constrained households and reshape the intergenerational distribution of homeownership.

Beyond Multipliers: Distributional Income Effects of Government Spending in the Euro Area

with Gökan Ider, Alexander Kriwoluzky, Melina Ludolph, and Lena Tonzer
Using harmonised micro-level data from the EU-SILC, this project examines how fiscal policy affects income growth across the income distribution, as well as across gender and occupational groups. We investigate the channels through which different types of government spending — consumption versus investment — transmit to household incomes.
